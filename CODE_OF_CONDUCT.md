# 🛡️ Code of Conduct

## 🤝 Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, maintainers, and community leaders pledge to make participation in the AOXChain community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to acting and interacting in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## ✅ Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- **Demonstrating empathy and kindness** toward other people.
- **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- **Giving and gracefully accepting** constructive feedback.
- **Accepting responsibility** for mistakes, apologizing to those affected, and learning from the experience.
- **Focusing on the collective good**, prioritizing what is best for the overall community, project integrity, and long-term collaboration.
- **Communicating professionally** in technical, operational, and social discussions.
- **Respecting boundaries** around time, expertise, privacy, and decision-making responsibility.

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing or threatening to publish another person’s private information, including physical or electronic addresses, without explicit permission.
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption of discussions.
- Bad-faith technical conduct, including manipulative review behavior, intentionally misleading claims, or hostile communication disguised as critique.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional, collaborative, or security-conscious setting.

---

## ⚖️ Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate, fair, and proportionate corrective action in response to behavior they determine to be inappropriate, threatening, offensive, abusive, or otherwise harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, pull requests, discussions, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

Enforcement decisions will prioritize community safety, project integrity, and a professional collaboration environment.

---

## 📍 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including but not limited to:

- source repositories,
- issues,
- pull requests,
- code reviews,
- discussions,
- documentation spaces,
- project chat channels,
- e-mail correspondence related to the project,
- official social media or public communication spaces.

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the AOXChain community in public spaces, including use of an official project e-mail address, official social media presence, or other formal representation.

---

## 📩 Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, discriminatory, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

> **Email:** [aoxchain@gmail.com](mailto:aoxchain@gmail.com)

When reporting an incident, please provide as much relevant context as possible, including:

- what happened,
- when it happened,
- where it happened,
- who was involved,
- links, screenshots, logs, or other supporting evidence if available.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

---

## 🔒 Privacy and Confidentiality

All community leaders handling reports are expected to respect the privacy, dignity, and security of the reporter, the affected individuals, and any witnesses.

Information shared through the reporting process will be handled with appropriate discretion and limited to those who need it for evaluation and response.

Retaliation against any individual for making a good-faith report is prohibited.

---

## 🛠️ Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders may apply the following enforcement measures, depending on the severity, pattern, intent, and impact of the conduct.

| Level | Community Impact | Consequence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Correction** | Use of inappropriate language or unprofessional behavior. | A private, written warning with clarification regarding the violation and expected future behavior. |
| **2. Warning** | A violation through a single incident or a pattern of inappropriate actions. | A formal warning with temporary restrictions on interaction or participation. |
| **3. Temporary Ban** | A serious violation or sustained inappropriate behavior. | A temporary ban from all community interaction for a defined period. |
| **4. Permanent Ban** | A pattern of violations, harassment, threats, or severe abuse. | A permanent ban from all public interaction within the community. |

The enforcement response may vary based on the full factual context and risk to the community.

---

## 🧭 Enforcement Principles

When evaluating incidents, community leaders may consider:

- the severity of the conduct,
- whether the behavior was repeated,
- whether the behavior appears intentional,
- whether a power imbalance was involved,
- the impact on the affected individual or broader community,
- the risk of recurrence,
- the need to preserve a safe and professional project environment.

Not all incidents require the same response, and enforcement may escalate when prior warnings or restrictions have been ignored.

---

## 🚫 Retaliation

Retaliation, intimidation, or hostile treatment directed at any person for:

- reporting a concern,
- participating in an investigation,
- providing relevant information,
- or supporting an affected individual

is considered a serious violation of this Code of Conduct.

Such behavior may result in escalated enforcement action independent of the original complaint.
